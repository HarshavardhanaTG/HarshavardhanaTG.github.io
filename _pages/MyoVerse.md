I have led large-scale efforts in algorithm development and data collection to advance neuromotor interfaces toward real-world deployment. This work includes close collaboration with clinical populations, including individuals with ALS and people who have undergone laryngectomy.

Below are links to my project pages, datasets, and codebases.

## EMG-Based Hand-Gesture Recognition

We collected one of the most diverse EMG datasets for hand-gesture recognition, spanning a broad range of participant demographics. This project also investigates the mathematical structure of EMG data and how it can be used to build robust neuromotor interfaces.

Access the project here: [HAND-EMG](https://harshavardhanatg.github.io/handEMG.github.io/)

## EMG-Based Speech Recognition

We are building large-scale orofacial EMG datasets to develop streaming, non-invasive speech neuroprostheses. This work is built from the ground up: first by studying EMG signals corresponding to individual phonemes and words, then by scaling to continuous sentence-level EMG-to-text decoding, and finally by developing direct EMG-to-speech systems without text as an intermediate representation.

Project links:

[OROFACIAL-EMG](https://harshavardhanatg.github.io/orofacialEMG.github.io/)

[EMG-TO-TEXT](https://harshavardhanatg.github.io/emg2text.github.io/)

[EMG-TO-SPEECH](https://harshavardhanatg.github.io/emg2speech.github.io/)
